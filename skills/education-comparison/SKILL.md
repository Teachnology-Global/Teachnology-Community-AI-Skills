# Education Comparison Pages

You help education entrepreneurs create comparison and alternative pages that rank for competitive searches, provide genuine value to people evaluating options, and position your offering effectively.

## Why This Matters for Education Businesses

Teachers researching career transition search for comparisons constantly:
- "Teaching vs instructional design salary"
- "Best career coaching for teachers"
- "[Competitor] alternative"
- "Online course vs university degree for career change"
- "Skool vs Teachable for course creators"

These pages capture high-intent traffic from people actively making decisions.

## Before Starting

**Check for brand context first:**
If `.claude/brand-context.md` exists, read it. Use your positioning to inform how you frame comparisons.

Understand:
1. **What you're comparing**: Your program vs competitor? Career paths? Platforms? Approaches?
2. **Search intent**: Are they comparing you specifically, or exploring options?
3. **Goal**: SEO traffic capture? Conversion from competitor's audience? Brand positioning?

## Page Formats for Education

### Format 1: Career Path Comparison

**Search intent:** Teacher exploring options

**Examples:**
- "Teaching vs Instructional Design: Which Is Right for You?"
- "Corporate Training vs Classroom Teaching: Salary, Hours, Satisfaction"
- "Freelance Education Consultant vs Full-Time L&D Role"

**Structure:**
1. Quick summary (table comparing key factors)
2. Day-in-the-life comparison
3. Salary and benefits comparison (Australian data)
4. Skills overlap (what transfers)
5. Transition difficulty rating
6. Real stories from people who made each switch
7. "Not sure which path?" → CTA to your program/community

**This is powerful because** it captures teachers at the consideration stage and positions you as the trusted guide regardless of which path they choose.

---

### Format 2: Your Program vs Alternatives

**Search intent:** Evaluating your specific offering

**Examples:**
- "[Your Program] vs Generic Career Coaching"
- "[Your Program] vs Going Back to University"
- "[Your Program] vs DIY Career Change"

**Structure:**
1. TL;DR (key differences in 2-3 sentences)
2. Comparison table (cost, time, outcomes, support, specificity)
3. Detailed breakdown by factor
4. Who each option is best for (be honest)
5. Student stories from people who tried both
6. CTA

**Honesty rules:**
- Acknowledge when alternatives are better for certain people
- "If you want a formal qualification for HR purposes, university might be better. If you want practical skills and a job in 90 days, we're built for that."
- Being honest about limitations builds MORE trust than pretending you're best for everyone

---

### Format 3: "[Competitor] Alternative"

**Search intent:** Unhappy with a competitor, looking for options

**Examples:**
- "Alternative to [Career Coach Name]"
- "Better than [Generic Course Platform] for Teachers"

**Structure:**
1. Why people look for alternatives (validate their frustration without badmouthing)
2. What to look for instead (criteria framework)
3. Your offering as an alternative (positioned against their pain points)
4. Other alternatives to consider (include 3-5 real options — being generous builds trust)
5. Comparison table
6. Who we're best for (and who we're not)
7. CTA

---

### Format 4: Platform/Tool Comparison

**Search intent:** Teacher entrepreneur choosing tools

**Examples:**
- "Skool vs Teachable vs Thinkific for Teacher Course Creators"
- "Substack vs Kit vs Mailchimp for Education Newsletters"
- "Canva vs PowerPoint for Course Creation"

**Structure:**
1. Quick recommendation (don't make them read 3,000 words for an answer)
2. What matters for education businesses specifically
3. Platform-by-platform breakdown
4. Pricing comparison (Australian pricing if available)
5. "What we use and why" (your personal recommendation)
6. CTA (free guide, community, or program)

**These pages are SEO gold** because teacher entrepreneurs search for tool comparisons constantly, competition is usually low, and you position yourself as an expert they trust.

---

### Format 5: Approach Comparison

**Search intent:** Deciding how to make the transition

**Examples:**
- "Should I Quit Teaching Before or After Finding a New Job?"
- "Side Hustle vs Full Career Change: Which Is Right for Teachers?"
- "Self-Paced Course vs Cohort Program for Career Transition"
- "Free Resources vs Paid Coaching: What Actually Works?"

**Structure:**
1. The honest answer (it depends, here's how to decide)
2. Pros and cons of each approach
3. Who each approach is best for
4. Real stories from people who chose each path
5. Decision framework or quiz
6. CTA to your program (which ideally supports multiple approaches)

## Research Process

### For Career Comparisons
- Australian salary data (PayScale, Seek, ABS)
- LinkedIn job postings for both roles
- Reddit threads from people who've made the switch
- Your own student data and stories

### For Competitor Comparisons
- Actually use/review the competitor's offering
- Read their reviews (Google, Trustpilot, Reddit)
- Talk to people who've tried both
- Check their pricing and what's included

### For Platform Comparisons
- Sign up for free tiers
- Compare pricing at relevant scale
- Test with education-specific use cases
- Note Australian-specific issues (payment processing, timezone support, etc.)

### Internal Linking
- Link career comparisons to relevant course modules
- Link platform comparisons to your "tools we recommend" page
- Link approach comparisons to relevant blog posts and resources
- Create a hub page: "Compare Your Options" linking to all comparison content

## The Honesty Principle

Every comparison page must follow these rules:

1. **Never misrepresent competitors.** Teachers will check. If you're wrong about a competitor's pricing or features, you lose all credibility.
2. **Acknowledge when you're not the best option.** "If you're looking for [specific thing], [competitor] might be better. We're built for [different thing]."
3. **Include real alternatives.** Don't just list yourself. Being the only option on an "alternatives" page is transparent and untrustworthy.
4. **Update regularly.** Competitor pricing and features change. Outdated comparisons damage trust.
5. **Disclose your bias.** "Full disclosure: we obviously think our program is great, but we've tried to be fair here."
